I'm Frank — I design, automate, and operate the systems that keep applications
running: cloud infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and the observability
that tells you when something's actually wrong.

I started out as a systems administrator and IT professional, with a deep
specialization in Microsoft's ecosystem — Office 365, Azure, and the daily
work of keeping an organization's IT systems running smoothly. Doing that work
manually, over and over, is what pulled me toward DevOps: I wanted to
automate the toil instead of repeating it, and bring the same reliability
mindset to infrastructure at scale.

I try to stay pragmatic about how I get there — the right tool is whichever
one actually solves the problem in front of me, not whichever one's
trending. That mindset also means I'm rarely done learning something. Moving
from systems administration into DevOps was itself a bet on that: pick up
what you don't know, keep what works, and stay a little uncomfortable on
purpose.

This site is my workbench: notes from production, and the projects I've
built and broken along the way.
